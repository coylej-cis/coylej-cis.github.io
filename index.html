The Problem We're Solving

Given that the height of the laser on the wall is \(8.0\) feet, the laser hits the ground \(8{\sqrt{3}}\) feet from the wall, and the wall is perpendicular to the floor, find the height of a person given their distance from the wall.

First, Angle A must be found. Then, we will use the measure of angle A and the length of the triangle's base. The method to find angle A is below:

\(tan (A)= opposite/adjacent\)

\(tan (A)= 8/13.85\)

\(A= arctan(8/13.85)\)

\(A = 30°\)

A Review of Some Important Topics Discussed on this Page

Trigonometric Functions

There are 6 trigonometric functions: sine, cosine, tangent, cosecant, secant, and cotangent. These functions are notated as sin, cos, tan, csc, sec, and cot, respectively. Each of these functions is a ratio of two sides of the triangle.


Inverse Trigonometric Functions

Each trigonometric function has an inverse function that works in reverse. Inverse functions are conveyed with the word “Arc” followed by a trigonometric function (arcsine). On most calculators, inverse functions are expressed as what looks like the trigonometric function to the power of negative one(Ex: \({cos}^{-1}(x)\) or \({sin}^{-1}(x)\)). While this form may look like an exponent, inverse trigonometric functions are not the same as trigonometric functions to the power of -1. These inverse functions are used to find the angle of a triangle by using 2 side lengths.
